## What this phase is

Two workstreams in one session.

### Workstream A — Staff experience

Today a STAFF login sees a diluted admin experience. STAFF-1 makes it a purpose-built
employee experience. **The STAFF home is the person-scoped, mobile-first `/my` portal**
— not the admin dashboard. HR-7 already built `/my/*` routes scoped to the signed-in
person's own records; audit what exists there and compose the staff home within it.

1. **Staff home (`/my`)**: replace the blank-card experience. Staff see Team Messages,
   Corporate Messages, a "My Compliance & Training" card (their open/unfinished
   compliance + training items, each with a direct link to complete it), and Instagram
   prominently. Nothing rendered may be an empty placeholder — every card has content
   or an intentional empty state.
2. **Checklists**: hidden for STAFF unless something is actually assigned to that
   staff member. Design the visibility check so it flips on when assignments exist.
3. **Store View**: not visible to STAFF.
4. **Inventory**: not visible to STAFF.
5. **HR nav renamed "My Documents" for STAFF only** (ADMIN/MANAGER keep "HR").
6. **Responsive**: genuinely usable on iPhone and tablet; staff will mostly use
   phones. Follow existing responsive patterns (e.g. store-view mobile treatment)
   rather than inventing new ones.
7. **BUG-1 completion**: whatever `6a14fb6` left unfinished for reliable dashboard
   card loading, finish it here (for all roles it touches, validated against STAFF).

### Workstream B — HR-11: DocuSign-style inline signing + real timestamp capture

Replace the HR-5-era signing interaction with a formal inline signing screen. **Design
constraint: formality and court-defensibility.** The signing moment must feel and
function like executing a legal document, not tapping a checkbox. **Expected to
require no schema changes** — the existing checkpoint/record structures should carry
the new capture; if not, present the case and stop.

Requirements (screen design still gets approved in Phase 1 before any build):

- **Review before sign**: the signer is presented the full document and must scroll
  or page through it before sign actions become available. Per-page initialing
  happens in sequence as the signer progresses — not as a single bulk action.
- **Per-interaction timestamp capture (fixes Defect 1 at the source):** each
  checkpoint records the actual moment the signer completed it, so the certificate
  shows a real human progression (17:21:04, 17:22:31, …) rather than 29 identical
  stamps. **Audit first:** determine whether per-interaction times are already
  captured client-side and flattened at save — if so, the fix is preserving them
  through persistence, not new capture. Report the finding in the Phase 1 plan.
- **Timestamp policy:** date+time is ALWAYS captured and stored. Rendering is an
  org-level display setting: signature validation stamps always show full date+time;
  inline date fills may render date-prominent with time subordinate.
  Court-defensibility wins all ties.
- **Consent statement**: consent text `esign-2026-07` already exists and is recorded
  on the certificate. HR-11 surfaces it ceremonially on the signing screen at the
  point of signature (not buried). **Fork for the Phase 1 plan:** keep the existing
  text as-is, or present a revised draft for the human to accept or replace with
  legal-supplied language.
- **Signature capture**: typed and/or drawn — propose which in the plan.
- **Attribution**: signer name, timestamp, and document version visible on the
  signing screen and embedded in the record.
- **Certificate continuity**: the Certificate of Acknowledgment (SHA-256 binding,
  append-only language, auth/IP/device capture) is preserved unchanged except that
  checkpoint rows now carry real per-interaction times.
- **Storage**: signed PDF flows through the existing architecture — a
  `FormSubmission` owns its own signed PDF; re-signing creates a new submission.
  Tommy's two coexisting records are the proof pattern; nothing may collapse,
  overwrite, or merge prior signed records.
- **Access**: staff view their signed documents in My Documents, but **signed-PDF
  download remains ADMIN/MANAGER-only** (departing employees must not self-download).
  Do not loosen this while building the staff-facing document list.
- **Out of scope (HR-11b):** anchor detection, anchor→mark mapping UI, inline page
  stamping, image-only-PDF fallback handling, and the `DocumentAnchor` schema case.
  If the audit surfaces anything useful to HR-11b (e.g. where page coordinates could
  be captured, how the PDF generation pipeline composes pages), write it down as text.
